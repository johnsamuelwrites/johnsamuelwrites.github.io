The Web is Dead..Again, Long Live the Web

John Samuel

NOTE: Article in Progress

The Web landscape is rapidly changing. Our behavoir on the web has changed over all these years. From web directories to web search engines, the way we navigate and search the internet for information has evolved. With the social media content behind the walled gardens, the web became more fragmented. From publicly available websites to content behind closed accounts, this evolution has also helped us to distinguish between public and private information. Yet another revolution is nigh. The arrival of Generative AI models has ignited serious debates on the web. Large language models and image (or media) generation models help produce content at a rapidly increasing rate. A new user behaviour seems to be emerging. The generation of personalized content generation to user search queries will mean that the users do not need to click external websites. What does it mean to the website owners, blog writers, and for the future of the web?

Changes.. and more changes

Looking back to the last three decades, we can clearly observe the evolution of the web. So have the search engines. But, what is the relation between the web and the search engines? Why do they need to be seen together? The decentralized nature of the web means that anyone, anywhere can create content on the web. However, these content need to be discovered. Unless some website links to them, these newly created websites will never be discovered. Throughout all these years, the search engines have played an important role in indexing almost all the websites on the web and played a role in helping users discover new content on the web. Initially, there were websites that maintained a directory of different categories of websites. Users could disvover the websites by going through all the websites of their preferred category.

However, as more and more organizations decided to create an online presence on the web, things become complicated. The search engines played an important role in helping users find relevant websites from thousands (or even millions) of websites. Users could make a search query on the search engines using some keywords From showing a list of ten search results with the now popular blue-green links, we ask our questions directly to the web Take, for example, when it comes to weather forecast, we type the 'city name temparature" and there we are with the results of the weather forecast on the same web page, without the need for clicking any links to the external website This simplified our lives. However, this also changed how we consume information on the web Search engines directly give us the results. We no longer click the links or use it less often.

Another change that we observed was the possibility to ask questions directly to the search engines with complete phrases No more keyword based search combinations. One could easily ask, "What's the temperature of Lyon"? The coming of AI is now going to make our lives simpler and we may now be able to ask more complex questions In fact, we may be able to converse to the search engines The search engines could guide us to the answers that we are looking for, all the time without leaving the website.

What about the sources? As the debate is raging all over the internet, how are these answers obtained. There are persons and organizations who put time and energy to prepare the content for their blogs and websites However, these sources are usually missing from these conversations It must be pointed out that these models may give references when we explicitly ask them Unless if they are hallucinating and coming out with some imaginary references.

However, my major concern is about the web. Yes, the web that I love. The vision of the decentralised web, where people could freely express their opinions, and cite other people's work with links, an interconnected web of information. That vision of the web is long gone and now replaced by walled gardens with no possibility to access the information easily without creating accounts. The preceding years have helped us understand the various nuances of open data access. We see people understanding the importance of privacy and asking for settings to manage private information. Not all information is meant for general public consumption. However, there are certain information that needs to be public and not behind the walled gardens, especially those related to public safety and health, official announcements.

With the advancement of the AI models, the access to the information for the general public is becoming more difficult Companies are making it difficult to access the data by making the API access available with a price tag. This is because the data was scrapped without their consent and the financial benefits do not reach the owners of these cmpanies Furthermore, some of the AI models do not suggest the source. Hence, the owners lose the traffic and thereby, the source of their income. Compared to the the social networking service, this situation is more grim since social networking services offered the possibility the users to share links.

Now we also hear about AI engine optimization, which means that in addition to the search engine optimization, the website owners need to optimize their content in order to ensure that their content appears in the responses of the AI models. Search engine optimization ensured that your website appears on the first page of the search engines for certain phrases, keywords typed by the users. The optimization of our websites for making sure that the company products appear in the suggested answers.

Is the web dead? Is it dead again? The web has siginificantly evolved in the last three decades. During the shortage of the IPv4 addresses The advent of the social media The advent of the mobile applications

Yet the web is still here. The way we interact with the web has changed what exactly is the web? The original source for the exchange of information in the form of pages We added more addresses to the web with IPv6. People and organizations still maintain blogs and websites. Focus from HTML to API with the arrival of mobile applications

Prediction

The web is going nowhere decentralized, commons, interoperability
