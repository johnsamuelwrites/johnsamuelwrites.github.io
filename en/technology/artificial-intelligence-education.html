Artificial Intelligence and Education

John Samuel

This article is part of a series on Artificial Intelligence.

This is a curated list of the impact of artificial intelligence on education, including research papers, articles, and resources that explore how AI is transforming the educational landscape. The list includes works that discuss the implications of AI in teaching, learning, and educational administration.

Articles

  • The AI Industry is Funding A Massive AI Training Initiative for Teachers : he integration of AI into education presents a complex dilemma: while some educators find AI tools beneficial for lightening workloads and personalizing instruction, others fear Big Tech's profit motives could jeopardize teaching jobs and hinder students' critical thinking skills.
  • Coding is dead: UW computer science program rethinks curriculum for the AI era Academic institutions are actively grappling with how to adapt curricula and teaching methods to prepare graduates for a workforce transformed by generative AI, focusing on AI literacy, critical thinking, and ethical considerations to bolster students against automation.

Opinions

  • AI is driving down the price of knowledge – universities have to rethink what they offerThe widespread accessibility of information due to generative AI is devaluing the "knowledge premium" historically offered by universities, putting pressure on tuition costs and graduate wages, and shifting the demand towards uniquely human skills, encapsulated in the C.R.E.A.T.E.R. framework (critical thinking, resilience, emotional intelligence, accountability/ethics, teamwork, entrepreneurial creativity, and reflection/lifelong learning).
  • Worried About AI? Study the HumanitiesWhile some tech leaders envision a technological singularity, the humanities, focused on the inherently human, offer a more optimistic future where human-centric roles will become increasingly vital as AI automates systematized tasks but fails to replicate genuine human connection and understanding.
  • 18 months. 12,000 questions. A whole lot of anxiety. What I learned from reading students’ ChatGPT logs Three male second-year undergraduates at a British university provided access to their shared ChatGPT Plus account. Their 18-month chat log contains nearly 12,000 prompts and responses, encompassing academic tasks, career counseling, mental health advice, and personal requests. This data offers insight into the students' varied use of AI in their daily lives.
