Artificial Intelligence and the Labor Market

John Samuel

This is a curated list of the impact of artificial intelligence on the labor market, focusing on the potential job displacement and creation due to AI advancements. The list includes various studies, articles, and reports that analyze the effects of AI on employment across different sectors. The sources cover a range of perspectives, from optimistic views on job creation to concerns about job displacement. The list is intended to provide a comprehensive overview of the current understanding of how AI is reshaping the labor market, highlighting both the opportunities and challenges that arise from the integration of AI technologies in various industries.

Impact of AI on Computer Science Education

  • Should Universities Raise or Lower Admission Requirements for CS Programs in the Age of GenAI? : Universities face a dilemma: should they raise, lower, or restructure computer science admission standards to adapt to the impact of generative AI on the field and attract a diverse range of skilled candidates? Universities should differentiate between research-focused programs emphasizing advanced problem-solving and foundational theories, and application-focused colleges concentrating on implementing existing AI tools and practical system integration.
  • The Computer-Science Bubble Is Bursting : After years of rapid expansion, computer science program enrollment nationwide has stalled, with some top universities like Stanford, Princeton, and Duke even reporting significant declines. The rise of AI is directly linked to increased unemployment in the tech sector, as companies like Microsoft and Anthropic openly state that AI tools are reducing their need for human coders, especially at entry levels.
